01 / Context

No design holding it together.

When a disaster hits — a flood, a fire, a supply chain collapse — most small and medium businesses in Latin America don't recover. Not because the knowledge to survive doesn't exist, but because it never reaches them in a usable form. Resiliencia en una Caja was built to change that: a structured learning platform, backed by CENACED, the ARISE Mx working group, and The UPS Foundation, offering ten modules designed to help SMEs prepare before the crisis arrives.


I joined the project after a first version had already launched. WordPress, Tutor LMS, an acquired theme customised through Elementor, video content produced by an external agency. The content was solid. What it didn't have was design — no visual identity, no clear user journey, no way to make a first-time visitor understand what the platform was or why they should trust it. My role was to build that layer from scratch.


My first two weeks were diagnostic. I installed Google Analytics, mapped the existing site structure, and ran through the self-diagnosis flow as a user. What I found: a few hundred visits, roughly ten completed self-assessments, zero coupon redemptions. The funnel existed on paper. In practice, no one was moving through it.
